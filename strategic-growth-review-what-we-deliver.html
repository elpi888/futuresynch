The Strategic Growth Review: What We Actually Deliver

It is not a discovery call. It is a written assessment that delivers real value before you commit to anything.

24 March 2026·5 min read·Future Synch

Most discovery calls are structured to sell a service. You explain your situation, we explain our services, and at the end one of us decides if there is a fit.

The Strategic Growth Review is structured differently. It is a paid written assessment — delivered before any ongoing engagement begins. You receive real analysis of your business. We commit our thinking before you commit your budget.

Section 1What It Is
The Written AssessmentStructure · Scope · Delivery Format
What You Receive

A structured written analysis of your business covering six domains: positioning and narrative clarity, go-to-market architecture, business development structure, operational discipline, capital strategy and investor narrative, and a 90-day structural action plan. Not a slide deck. Not a summary call. A document you can act on immediately.

How It Works

You complete a structured intake form covering your stage, your constraints, your current channels, your stack, and your capital situation. We review it and deliver a written assessment within five business days. No calls until you have read the assessment and decided it is worth continuing.

We deliver £2,500 of work. You commit. The difference is our stake in your success.

Section 2Who It Is For
The Right FitStage · Readiness · Commitment
Who Gets the Most From It

Founders with traction who are operating in complexity — scaling past early customers, preparing for capital conversations, or restructuring a go-to-market that is not converting. Teams that have a real business and want structural clarity, not motivational advice.

Who It Is Not For

Idea-stage exploration without commitment, founders looking for free strategy calls, or teams that want validation rather than analysis. The review is built for implementation — not reflection. If you are not ready to act on the findings within 90 days, the timing is wrong.

Section 3What the 90 Days Look Like
After the AssessmentImplementation · Engagement Options · Structural Work
The Assessment as a Foundation

The Strategic Growth Review is a standalone deliverable — you own it and can implement the findings independently. It is also the foundation for any ongoing engagement. We do not enter ongoing work without completing the review first. The assessment is how we verify that the fit is real on both sides.

Engagement Options

After the review, founders who want ongoing support can engage on a 90-day structured programme — either Done With You (£9,000) or Done For You (£15,000). Both are structured around the findings of the assessment. Nothing is generic.

If Structuring the Next 90 Days
01

Complete the intake form honestly. The quality of the assessment depends on the quality of the input. The form asks specific questions about constraints, failures, and current priorities — not just the highlights.

02

Read the assessment before we speak. The assessment is delivered in writing first. Read it before we schedule any follow-up. This ensures the conversation is about implementation, not explanation.

03

Identify your top three action items. The assessment will include a prioritised 90-day plan. Before any follow-up, identify which three items you will act on regardless of whether we work together. That is the readiness signal.

04

Decide on implementation support. Some founders implement independently. Others want ongoing support. Both are valid. The assessment gives you what you need to make that decision from an informed position.

ConclusionReal Value Before You Commit
Strategic Growth Summary

The review is the commitment we make first — not the contract you sign.

Most agencies ask you to commit before they deliver value. The Strategic Growth Review inverts that. You receive a genuine written analysis of your business — specific, structural, and actionable — before any ongoing engagement begins.

If the findings are useful and the fit is real, we continue. If not, you have a strategic document that is worth the investment regardless.
